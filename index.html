Artifact 1

BridgeAI

Enterprise Business Analyst Copilot

Workshop 1 – AI Lab Artifact

BridgeAI is an AI-powered assistant designed to support Business Analysts throughout the software development lifecycle. It helps improve productivity by assisting with communication, documentation, analysis, and decision support while encouraging responsible AI use and human oversight.

This artifact was developed during Workshop 1 of my Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning coursework and was selected because it demonstrates the practical application of AI to a real Business Analysis challenge.

Problem

Business Analysts often spend significant time creating documentation, organizing requirements, and communicating information across stakeholder groups.

Solution

A focused AI copilot that supports requirements work, meeting summaries, user stories, process analysis, stakeholder communication, and structured decision support.

Professional Value

Artifact 2

Cybersecurity & Data Privacy

AI Approaches in Cybersecurity Operations

AI & Machine Learning Presentation

This team presentation examines how traditional machine learning and deep learning are used in cybersecurity operations. It compares Cloudflare's real-time bot detection with Microsoft Security Copilot and evaluates technical fit, cost, explainability, privacy, regulatory requirements, and responsible AI adoption.

Problem

Cybersecurity teams must analyze large volumes of network, identity, endpoint, and threat-intelligence data while reducing false positives and protecting sensitive data.

Analysis

The artifact compares CatBoost-based traditional machine learning with transformer-based deep learning and explains why each approach fits a different cybersecurity use case.

Professional Value

This work demonstrates research, technical comparison, ethical reasoning, collaboration, data privacy awareness, and professional presentation skills.

Topics Covered

Cybersecurity data landscape Traditional machine learning Deep learning and transformers Cloudflare bot detection Microsoft Security Copilot Responsible AI adoption Data privacy and regulation Cost and ethical trade-offs

My Contribution

I contributed as the Industry Analyst by researching the cybersecurity industry, identifying major data types and operational challenges, and connecting those findings to the use of AI and machine learning in modern security operations.

What I Learned

This artifact helped me understand that the most advanced model is not always the best choice. Traditional machine learning may be more appropriate when speed, interpretability, and auditability are priorities, while deep learning is valuable for complex, unstructured, and context-dependent security signals.
